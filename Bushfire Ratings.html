Bushfire Ratings

The New Fire Rating System

If you know the changes; it could save your life.

No Rating

Be Aware Of

The Conditions

On days with minimal risk of a fire occuring, the fire danger rating will be set to 'No Rating'. Bushfires lit in this environment will be extremely unlikely to get out of control and cause a risk to homes and lives.

Moderate

Start To

Plan and Prepare

Check your bushfire plan is up to date with your family and is not out of date. Stay alert for fires in your area so you can be one step ahead.

High

Be Ready

To Act

With this rating shown, fires that are lit can threaten lives and homes and you should be prepared to leave the bushfire risk area, if needed with all animals and valuables.

Extreme

Take Action Now

Fires in your area with an Extreme fire danger rating will have a likely chance to lives and homes and It is advised that you leave your home if you are in the bushfire prone area, if possible take all animals with you.

Catastrophic

Leave Your

House Immediately

These conditions occur when there is low humidity and it is extremely hot in your area. These conditions are the most dangerous for a bushfire. Homes cannot withstand the heat and intensity of this type of blaze. You must leave your home IMMEDIATELY and get to a safe and secure location like an evacuation point out of the fire prone area.

Summer

Summer time is the most deadliest time for bushfires with the low humidity and extremely high temperatures. The hotter and the higher the humidity the easier it is for a bushfire to go out of control. With this sort of temperature, a bushfire can escalate extremely quickly and can brings homes, lives, and towns to the threat of everything burning to the ground.

Autumn

In Autumn, the temperature descalates and there it is more humid in the air so if a fire does start it will not be as hard to control as a fire in Summer. Most fires in Autumn might be a threat to lives and homes

Winter

During Winter, is the unrestriced season where you can burn large piles of leaf litter without needed a permit. If a fire does escape, it will either extinguish because of rain or the fuel it is trying to burn is extremely wet. This time of the year is the easiest time to burn because of large amounts of rain and low temperatures.

Spring

During Spring, if you have a large enough property you will need to put in a firebreak. A firebreak is a 5 metre break from the edge of you property to give a small break where the fire cannot reach the property in that 5 metre break becaue their is no fuel there. You can still burn in the unrestricted season but at the begining of October you will have to have a permit issued by a Fire Control Officer at your local fire brigade to burn leaves or bush in your property.
